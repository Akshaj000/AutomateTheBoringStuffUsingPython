4.What is an expression made up of? What do all expressions do?

 An expression is a combination of operators and operands that is interpreted to produce some other value.
 In any programming language, an expression is evaluated as per the precedence of its operators. So that if there is more than one operator in an expression, 
 their precedence decides which operation will be performed first.
 Expressions are just values combined with operators, and they always evaluate down to a single value.
 Python expressions contain identifiers, literals, and operators.
